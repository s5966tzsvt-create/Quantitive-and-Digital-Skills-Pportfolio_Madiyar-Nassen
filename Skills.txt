Skills Developed – Project 1: Portfolio & Investment Analysis

During this project I developed a range of quantitative and digital skills
applicable to professional investment analysis. I sourced daily adjusted
closing price data for two large-cap technology stocks — Alphabet Inc.
(GOOGL) and Meta Platforms Inc. (META) — from Yahoo Finance, and organised
the raw data into a structured, professional Excel workbook. I calculated
daily arithmetic returns using the formula (Pt − Pt−1) / Pt−1 and geometric
returns using the natural logarithm method LN(Pt) − LN(Pt−1), understanding
the distinction between the two approaches and when each is appropriate.

I computed a full set of descriptive statistics including mean, median,
standard deviation, variance, mean absolute deviation, interquartile range,
and min/max values using Excel functions such as AVERAGE, STDEV.S, VAR.S,
AVEDEV, and PERCENTILE.INC. I applied z-score analysis to identify potential
outliers in the return distributions, flagging observations where |Z| > 3
as per standard statistical practice.

To assess the relationship between the two stocks, I calculated the sample
covariance both manually — by computing the product of deviations from the
mean for each observation and summing across the sample — and using Excel's
COVARIANCE.S function, verifying that both methods produce identical results.
I then derived the Pearson correlation coefficient using the CORREL function
and interpreted the result in the context of portfolio diversification.

I applied mean-variance analysis and calculated the Sharpe Ratio — both on
a daily basis and annualised by multiplying by the square root of 252 trading
days — using a risk-free rate based on the U.S. one-year Treasury bill yield.
This allowed me to compare the risk-adjusted performance of the two stocks
on a like-for-like basis.

Finally, I structured the spreadsheet to professional standards with clear
sheet organisation, consistent formatting, and embedded charts, and uploaded
the project to a GitHub repository as part of my digital portfolio. These
exercises strengthened my ability to apply statistical reasoning to real
financial data and communicate analytical findings in a clear, structured,
and professional format suitable for presentation to a client or employer.
